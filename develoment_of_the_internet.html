1950s

Sputnik was launched in 1957 by the Soviet Union.

It was the first satellite to orbit earth and was the size of a basketball.

It was used to transmit noises to earth through the radio.

NASA was then formed and technology and satellites began to develop.
(national geographic 2024)

1960s

A network of computers started to develop using Packet Switching.

This is the transferring of small pieces of data using networks.

Arpanet was the first ever network developed in the US.

The computers communicated using the network and through an operating system called Unix.  

1970s

 

Arpanet developed and made a connection from the US to London.

France also developed their own network called Cyclades.

TCP/IP was developed to link networks together.

Emails and PCS (Personal Communication Service) were also introduced.

1980s

 

Arpanet switched over to TCP/IP and the users grew.

Domain names were made more user friendly to help with internet searches.

Tim Berners Lee came up with the world wide web having previously called it Mesh.

In 1989 Apple pulled out of the AppleLink. It was renamed to America online.

1990s

In 1990 Tim Berners Lee share the first photo on the internet with the singers know as Les Horrible Crenettes.

The Arpanet network was cancelled.

Dial up internet connection was used commercially.

Search engines were created and were called the Archie Index along with the first web page explaining what the world wide web is. Google was created in 1998.

Ecommerce began with the first ever online transaction being the purchase of a CD in 1994.

WLAN (Wireless Local Area Network) was developed. 

The first mobile phone with internet was created in 1996 by Nokia.

2000s

The was a huge development and shift in technology.

Social media was developed.

The iPhone was unveiled. 

Streaming and on demand services were introduced.

There was an advancement in communication methods such as video calls through Skype.

YouTube was launched in 2005 bringing free online video hosting and sharing to the masses.

In 2006 Twitter was launched originally going to be called twitter (inspired by Flickr). The first Twitter message was “just setting up my twitter”

2010s

Rise in social media platforms. Instant messaging and video sharing gains rapid popularity.    

Online steaming demand increased.

Banking came online.

Assistive technology was introduced to cater for individual needs.

2020s

The global pandemic highlighted the reliance on the internet. Education and employment became remote.

Artificial intelligence was introduced in an instant computerised format. 

The National Broadband Plan is rolled out in Ireland.

5G network is also created.
